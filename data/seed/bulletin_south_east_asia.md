## Scope

This bulletin summarises antimicrobial resistance among invasive isolates reported by the South-East Asia sentinel network between 2018 and 2024. Percentages are the share of tested invasive isolates classified as non-susceptible under EUCAST breakpoints. Blood and cerebrospinal fluid isolates only; screening swabs are excluded, which means these figures understate colonisation pressure.

## Klebsiella pneumoniae — carbapenems

Non-susceptibility rose from 31.7% in 2018 to 42.0% in 2024, a change of +10.3 percentage points. The 2024 figure rests on 3381 tested isolates.

Year-by-year: 2018: 31.7%; 2019: 33.0%; 2020: 35.9%; 2021: 36.7%; 2022: 39.6%; 2023: 40.8%; 2024: 42.0%.

A change of this size over six years is unlikely to be explained by testing-practice drift alone. The network attributes it primarily to clonal expansion within hospital settings rather than to community transmission, though the surveillance design cannot separate the two cleanly.
