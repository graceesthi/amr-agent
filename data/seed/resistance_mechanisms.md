## Carbapenemases

Carbapenem non-susceptibility in Enterobacterales arises through several
distinct mechanisms, and the distinction matters for interpreting trends.
Carbapenemase production — KPC, OXA-48-like, NDM, VIM — is plasmid-borne and
therefore transmissible between strains and species. Non-carbapenemase
mechanisms, chiefly extended-spectrum beta-lactamase or AmpC production combined
with porin loss, are not horizontally transmissible in the same way.

A rise driven by carbapenemase spread and a rise driven by porin loss look
identical in a percentage-based surveillance series but demand different
responses: the former is an infection-control problem, the latter a prescribing
problem. Surveillance that reports only percentages cannot distinguish them.

## Meticillin resistance in Staphylococcus aureus

Meticillin resistance is mediated by mecA or mecC carriage. Unlike the
Enterobacterales picture, the trend across most European reporting networks has
been downward since the mid-2000s, attributed to hand-hygiene programmes and
active surveillance cultures. The decline has flattened in several networks,
which is consistent with those interventions having reached their ceiling rather
than with a new driver appearing.

## Vancomycin resistance in enterococci

vanA and vanB mediated resistance in Enterococcus faecium has risen in several
networks against the general downward trend in Gram-positive resistance. The
increases reported are concentrated in haematology and transplant units, and
clonal outbreak investigation has accounted for a substantial share of the
observed rise — meaning the aggregate regional figure can move sharply on the
strength of one or two hospital outbreaks.
