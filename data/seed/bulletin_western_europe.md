## Scope

This bulletin summarises antimicrobial resistance among invasive isolates reported by the Western Europe sentinel network between 2018 and 2024. Percentages are the share of tested invasive isolates classified as non-susceptible under EUCAST breakpoints. Blood and cerebrospinal fluid isolates only; screening swabs are excluded, which means these figures understate colonisation pressure.

## Klebsiella pneumoniae — carbapenems

Non-susceptibility rose from 4.8% in 2018 to 9.3% in 2024, a change of +4.5 percentage points. The 2024 figure rests on 4029 tested isolates.

Year-by-year: 2018: 4.8%; 2019: 5.1%; 2020: 7.4%; 2021: 6.9%; 2022: 7.4%; 2023: 8.5%; 2024: 9.3%.

Non-susceptibility rose from 7.1% in 2018 to 18.3% in 2024, a change of +11.2 percentage points. The 2024 figure rests on 2869 tested isolates.

Year-by-year: 2018: 7.1%; 2019: 9.8%; 2020: 10.7%; 2021: 12.4%; 2022: 15.3%; 2023: 16.9%; 2024: 18.3%.

A change of this size over six years is unlikely to be explained by testing-practice drift alone. The network attributes it primarily to clonal expansion within hospital settings rather than to community transmission, though the surveillance design cannot separate the two cleanly.
